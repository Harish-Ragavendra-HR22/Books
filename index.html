First Lie Wins

In "First Lie Wins" by Ashley Elston, a group of friends navigates a web of secrets and deception during their senior year of high school. When a lie spirals out of control, they face the fallout while dealing with friendships, budding romances, and the pressure of adulthood. This compelling story blends mystery and coming-of-age themes, revealing how one small lie can lead to significant consequences.

Just for the Summer

In "Just for the Summer," the third book in the Part of Your World series by Abby Jimenez, Emma, a traveling nurse, unexpectedly finds herself in Minnesota for a summer assignment. Teaming up with Justin, a man cursed to have every woman he dates find true love after their breakup, they initially plan to date and part ways, but their journey reveals deeper feelings and the possibility that they might be each other’s true match.

When the Ice Is Gone

When the Ice Is Gone" by Paul Bierman explores the transformative power of climate change through the author’s experiences in the Arctic. By combining personal narrative with scientific insights, Bierman highlights the effects of melting ice on ecosystems and communities while emphasizing the urgency of addressing climate change's profound implications for the planet's future.

Love Requires Chocolate

In "Love Requires Chocolate" by Ravynn K. Stringfield, a young woman navigates the complexities of love, friendship, and self-discovery, with chocolate symbolizing both the sweetness and challenges of relationships. Through humor and heartfelt moments, she learns that love, like chocolate, requires patience, vulnerability, and indulgence, ultimately revealing the importance of embracing her true self.
